Prime Tech is a personal rebranding project for a client seeking a full transformation of their existing PC brand, formerly known as EdgePC. The main deliverable was a complete rebrand — name, logo, and website. I came up with the name Prime Tech to represent being the best in the tech industry. Packaging Design

Lemon Twirl is a fictional cracker brand created as a packaging design project. The main deliverable was to develop a new creative design, die-lines, and a production-ready file for a cracker. Inspired by the bright, zesty flavor, the design uses playful character illustrations and warm gradients to reflect a fun, approachable identity. The packaging balances clarity and personality, featuring clean layouts, custom type styling, and bilingual content to reflect a Canadian market.
Maplore(Pronounced: "Mah-plor-ay") is a non-alcoholic beverage concept rooted in Canadian identity with a subtle French influence—hence the accented “é”. The design is clean and minimal, featuring a bold maple leaf as the focal point. Created to feel premium yet approachable, the Maple Golden Peach flavor highlights a natural, refined sweetness that reflects both the product and its heritage.

Personal Branding

Personal Branding is a project focused on creating a visual identity that reflects my values as a graphic and web designer. The main deliverable was a personal logo and brand / style guide. The concept combines a stylized ‘J’ with a handshake, symbolizing both my initials and a collaborative spirit. I explored minimal, geometric forms and a bold three-tone palette to communicate professionalism with a creative edge. The brand system was built to be flexible and consistent across digital and print touch points.

Circo Corazon

The main deliverable:
To design an original typeface from scratch.

What I delivered:
I created Circo Corazón, a custom display typeface that merges playful shapes with romantic curves.

The name is a blend of two words:
“Corazón” – Spanish for heart, reflecting the affectionate, soft curves of each letterform.
“Circo” – from the Italian word for circle, hinting at the rounded, fluid motion of the design.

The result is a whimsical yet structured alphabet that feels both dynamic and elegant. Its unique heart-inspired curves and circular strokes make it perfect for expressive titles, posters, and editorial use.
